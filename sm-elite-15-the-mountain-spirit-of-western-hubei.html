Synopsis

民国三年（1914年）8月，湖北西部，姊陵坪。

早在清末，就有人提出修筑连接湖北、四川两省的"川汉铁路"，民间集资成立铁路公司，在湖北境内沿途勘察。清宣统二年（1910年），一名教授来到鄂西"姊陵坪"考察地貌，那里的管理者是拥有大片土地的"巴康寨"寨主，以及世代供奉"山灵"、相传可以掌控生死的"巫师"。

民国三年（1914年）6月，詹天佑担任北洋政府交通部"汉粤川铁路督办"后，组建测量队，分头对湖北至四川的路段进行复勘。8月，一位年轻的专家带助手来到"姊陵坪"勘察，不知道这一次，又将是怎样的结果……

August, Year 3 of the Republic of China (1914). Zilíng Píng, western Hubei.

As far back as the late Qing dynasty, there were proposals to build a railway linking Hubei and Sichuan provinces. A privately funded rail company was established and began surveying the route through Hubei. In 1910, a professor arrived in the western Hubei settlement of Ziling Ping to survey the terrain — a place governed by the headman of Bakang Village, who held vast tracts of land, and by a hereditary shaman said to commune with the "Mountain Spirit" and hold power over life and death.

In June 1914, Zhan Tianyou was appointed Superintendent of the Han-Yue-Chuan Railway by the Beiyang government, and dispatched survey teams to re-examine the Hubei–Sichuan sections. In August, a young expert arrived in Ziling Ping with an assistant to conduct the survey. What this visit will bring, no one yet knows…
